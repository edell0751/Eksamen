Accessibility

Starting phase

Once we started planning our project and the layout of our web-page, we addressed the key factors our design would rely on. Our aim was to create a website that fullfills criterias such as clearity, accessibility and creativity. We also wanted to make our website more modern, with other color-paletts as we found the regular yellow, black and white palette boring. to make it more modern we decided to use a more dark color and contrast it with a darker shade of white and other colors, because too much white could be tiring for the users eye. another key part of our planning was to make it as accessible as possible. We came with the conclusion to do this through avoid using pixels as much as possible, and instead take usage of more scale-friendly measures such as rem,vh, percentages and vw.

As we were working

Throughout the time we were developing our websites, did we always try to keep the site well structured and clearly visible. we did this through color coding our website to make it more accessible. for instance we used a pink/orange color on our action buttons, and around elements which would react once you pressed them. this makes the user aware that once you press the button, link or video, the page would react. we also got recomended by our mentors to break up the structure a bit to make it look more modern and interesting, and thats why we chose to design the "studiehistorier" section the way we did. we wanted to break the flow and remove the centering for this section to add a little of creativity aswell. we chose to use a bit of javascript in our coding to make the site smoother once you press buttons. we did also run our site through Axe Accessibility Linter Tool´s extension on VS code, and Wave WEB Accessibility Evaluation Tool as a web extension. the results gave us feedback on what we needed to change to make the site more accessible and user-friendly.

Our experience working in github

Our experience is that GitHub has been a really usefull and powerful tool when cooperating with other developers. The process has for the most part been pretty straight forward, with the exception of a few hiccups here and there. Mostly merge related problems.

Short list of benefits while wokring in GitHub

  • Easy to cooporate

  • Branches made distributing work easier

  • GitHub pages made it possible to problem search our website with tools such as Wave

  • Intuitive and clear way of showing changes made
